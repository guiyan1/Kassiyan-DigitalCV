Utilizing Remote Sensing and GIS to analyze Pasture Biomass based on the RADE farmlet

This research aimed to develop an efficient method for analyzing pasture biomass by integrating GIS and Remote Sensing technologies based on the RADE farmlet. By leveraging Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV)-based RS methods, this study sought to optimize pasture management by providing a better understanding of the resource base, facilitating analysis at enhanced spatial and temporal resolutions.

  • Utilized UAV-based RS imagery captured by a MicaSense RedEdge-M (Legacy) multispectral camera mounted on a DJI Phantom 4 Pro V2.0 UAV, combined with pasture yield measurements collected in the field over four seasons covering one year.
  • Explored the relationship between the NDVI obtained from the multispectral camera and pasture dry matter yield, implementing a regression model for predicting future pasture biomass levels.
  • Examined additional factors and used UAV-based point clouds to estimate sward height, improving year-round pasture management.
